Detailed Biography & Life Journey

An extensive overview of the life, character, and mission of the Final Prophet (ﷺ)

👶 Early Life, Lineage, and Character (570 – 610 CE)

Hazrat Muhammad ﷺ was born in Mecca in 570 CE into the noble Hashim clan of the Quraish tribe. His father, Abdullah, passed away before his birth, and his mother, Aminah, passed away when he was only six years old. Raised initially by his grandfather Abdul-Muttalib and later by his protective uncle Abu Talib, his upbringing amidst hardship molded a deeply compassionate and resilient character.

In his youth, he worked as a shepherd and later as a trustworthy merchant. Throughout Mecca, long before his prophethood, he earned the titles Al-Amin (The Trustworthy) and As-Sadiq (The Truthful).

📖 The First Revelation & Early Preaching in Mecca (610 – 622 CE)

Seeking solace from the moral decay of pre-Islamic Arabian society, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ frequently retreated to the Cave of Hira on Mount An-Nour. At the age of 40, during the month of Ramadan, Angel Jibreel (Gabriel) appeared to him with the divine command: "Read in the name of your Lord who created..." marking the beginning of the Quranic revelation.

🐪 The Migration (Hijrah) & Establishment of Madinah (622 – 630 CE)

In 622 CE, following divine guidance, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ migrated from Mecca to Yathrib (renamed Al-Madinah Al-Munawwarah). This event, known as the Hijrah, marks the start of the Islamic calendar. In Madinah, he drafted the historic Constitution of Madinah.

❤️ Liberation of Mecca & The Farewell Sermon (630 – 632 CE)

In 630 CE, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ marched toward Mecca with an army of 10,000. Entering the city without bloodshed, he announced a general amnesty for his former tormentors, declaring: "Go, for you are free." During his Farewell Pilgrimage in 632 CE, he delivered his legendary Farewell Sermon, emphasizing universal human equality and rights.
